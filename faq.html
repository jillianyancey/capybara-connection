FAQ

Have questions before your visit? We’re here to help with everything you need to know to plan a smooth, fun day at the farm.

Visitor Information

Tickets & Visits

What types of tickets are available?

We offer tickets for Private Capybara Encounters and individual, family/group, and Sensory Tuesday tickets for Capybara Commons. We also offer season passes for frequent visitors. You can see full details and pricing on our Plan Your Visit page.

Do I need a reservation?

Reservations are recommended for private capybara encounters, Sensory Tuesday sessions, and large group visits. General petting farm tickets can usually be purchased at the door, but space may be limited during peak times.

Can I bring my own food or drinks?

Outside food and drinks are not allowed in the animal areas. We have a small café with snacks and drinks available for visitors.

Are any of the experiences sensory-friendly?

Yes! Sensory Tuesday sessions are designed for guests who prefer quieter, low-stimulation visits. Attendance is limited, and noise is kept to a minimum.

What is the age range for visitors?

Guests of all ages are welcome! Children should be supervised, and all visitors should follow staff instructions for interacting safely with animals.

Animals & Interaction

Are capybaras friendly?

Yes! Capybaras are social, gentle animals. Our herd is used to visitors and enjoys supervised interaction, including petting and gentle feeding.

Can I hold or pick up a capybara?

For the safety of both visitors and animals, capybaras are not held. You can pet them gently while seated or standing during supervised sessions.

What other animals can I see at the facility?

In addition to capybaras, we have goats of various breeds, a small flock of sheep, and a group of friendly rabbits. All animals are accustomed to visitors and can be petted under staff supervision.

Where do capybaras come from?

Capybaras are native to South America, where they are found in nearly every country except Chile. They thrive in tropical rainforests , savannas, and wetlands, always living near water sources like lakes, rivers, swamps, and marshes.
